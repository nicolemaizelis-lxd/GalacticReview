Grade 7 - Measuring Cosmic Distances

**Objective:** Learn to represent and compare whole numbers up to one billion, including using powers of ten.

Concept: The Power of Ten

When numbers get very large, we use a shortcut called "powers of ten." Instead of writing out `1,000,000,000`, we can write it as 109. The little number (the exponent) tells us how many times to multiply 10 by itself. It also tells us how many zeros are after the 1!

  • 101 = 10
  • 102 = 10 × 10 = 100
  • 103 = 10 × 10 × 10 = 1,000 (One thousand)
  • 106 = 1,000,000 (One million)
  • 109 = 1,000,000,000 (One billion)

Example 1: Planetary Populations

The population of Earth is approximately 8,000,000,000 people.

  • In powers of ten, we can write this as 8 × 109.
  • To read this, we say "eight billion."

Example 2: Distance to the Moon

The average distance from Earth to the Moon is about 384,400 kilometers.

  • We can approximate this using powers of ten: 3.844 × 105 km.
  • If we round it to the nearest hundred thousand, it's 400,000 km, which is 4 × 105 km.
